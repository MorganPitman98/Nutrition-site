Macronutrients

Carbohydrates

Carbs provide 4 calories per gram. They’re your body's primary source of energy, especially during workouts. Found in fruit, rice, oats, pasta, and bread.

Proteins

Proteins contain 4 calories per gram. They’re crucial for muscle repair, recovery, and growth. Key sources: chicken, beef, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes.

Fats

Fats deliver 9 calories per gram. They help with hormone production, nutrient absorption, and long-term energy. Healthy sources: olive oil, nuts, seeds, avocado, oily fish.

Vitamins & Minerals

Vitamin A

Supports vision, skin health, and immune function. Found in carrots, sweet potatoes, and spinach.

Vitamin C

Boosts the immune system and helps with iron absorption. Found in citrus fruits, peppers, and broccoli.

Vitamin D

Important for bone health and testosterone support. Found in fatty fish, egg yolks, and sunlight.

Iron

Vital for oxygen transport and energy. Found in red meat, lentils, and leafy greens.

Magnesium

Supports muscle recovery and sleep. Found in nuts, whole grains, and dark chocolate.

Zinc

Boosts immunity and testosterone. Found in meat, shellfish, and seeds.

Glycogen

Glycogen is the stored form of carbohydrates in your muscles and liver. During training, especially weightlifting and high-intensity workouts, your body taps into glycogen for fast energy. Replenishing glycogen post-workout with carbs helps muscle recovery and performance.

Healthy Eating Tips

  • Prioritize whole foods over processed ones
  • Stay consistent with meals and hydration
  • Fuel around training: carbs before, protein after
  • Balance your plate: protein, carbs, fat, and color
  • Don’t fear fats — healthy ones are essential
